Our History

Samsung: A Legacy of Evolution and Innovation
Founded in 1938 by the visionary Lee Byung-Chull, our company emerged as a modest grocery trading store in Taegu, Korea. Lee embarked on his entrepreneurial journey by trading locally produced goods, such as noodles and tofu, before expanding into international markets. It was during this time, the name "Samsung" was chosen, meaning "Three Stars" in Korean. After the Korean War, the company diversified into textiles, establishing the largest woolen mill in Korea.

Driven by a relentless pursuit of progress, Samsung made a transformative decision to enter the realm of electronics three decades later. The company's initial foray into this domain centered around the production of black-and-white televisions, establishing the Samsung name as a significant manufacturer in Korea. Rapid international expansion followed, as our diverse range of products captured the attention of consumers worldwide.

In the early 1980s, the world witnessed the exponential growth of Samsung's technology business. Recognizing the escalating demand for system development in the business world, Samsung Data Systems was established, catering to the evolving need.

Fast forward to the present day, with a new generation of leadership led by Lee's son, Samsung unveiled the groundbreaking Samsung Galaxy Smartphone series. Our unwavering commitment to innovation propelled the company into other electronic realms, including smartwatches and home appliances. Today, Samsung stands as an industry pioneer, continuously pushing the boundaries of technology and shaping the future of innovation.

With a remarkable journey spanning over eight decades, Samsung's legacy is one of evolution, adaptation, and transformative advancements. As we look to the future, we remain dedicated to create meaningful and innovative solutions that enrich the lives of individuals worldwide.
